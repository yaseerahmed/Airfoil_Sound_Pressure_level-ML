Airfoil Analysis

Airfoil also spelled Aerofoil, shaped surface, such as an airplane wing, tail, or propeller blade, that produces lift and drag when moved through the air. An airfoil produces a lifting force that acts at right angles to the airstream and a dragging force that acts in the same direction as the airstream.
  • The angle of attack (AOA) is the angle at which the chord of an aircraft's wing meets the relative wind
  • The chord length is the distance between the trailing edge and the point where the chord intersects the leading edge
  • Free stream velocity is the air far upstream of an aerodynamic body, that is, before the body has a chance to deflect, slow down or compress the air.
